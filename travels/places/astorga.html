Astorga

Fernando, CC BY-SA 4.0 , via Wikimedia Commons

Astorga is a town in north Spain. It’s at the crossroads of the Camino de Santiago pilgrims’ route and the Roman Silver Road, and known for its medieval, walled Old Town. The Episcopal Palace was designed by modernist Antonio Gaudí and houses Los Caminos Museum, with religious artifacts. The Gothic Astorga Cathedral has a Gaspar Becerra altarpiece. The Museo del Chocolate covers the local chocolate-making industry.
