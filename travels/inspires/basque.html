Explore the Basque Coast

Catch a train from Montparnasse Station down to the perfect place to start a discovery of Basque Country.

The ideal introduction to what makes the Basque country (on both sides of the border) great. Great food, great architecture, fascinating history and plenty of opportunities to immerse yourself.

Take the train, bus or even walk to the twin-town Biarritz where you can catch some surf, or explore the genteel parts and find out why Edward VII liked to spend so much time here.

Another great resort with a great beach - compare and contrast with Biarritz. Other options are Hendaye and Hondarribia.

Being just 20km from the French border, San Sebastian (Donostia in Basque) is an ideal hop off when travelling to Spain. Where better to top up your tan, fill up your tummy and empty your wallet in this seductively urbane resort? Probably the most difficult decision you will have to make here is which of the beaches to choose from?

An authentic Basque town off the tourist trail. Why not while away an hour or two wandering around the historic quarter with it's neat Basque townhouses and the odd grand pile like the high-gothic church of Santa María which leads to the covered market place for rainy days.

Capital of the Basque Autonomous Community. In the medieval quarter, the Gothic-style Santa María Cathedral features a sculpted facade and towering columns.

Sometimes referred to as the City of the Guggenheim, what better place to take in culture, history and fine food.
